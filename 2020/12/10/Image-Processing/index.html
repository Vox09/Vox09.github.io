Logic operators

  2. OR. union.
  3. AND. conjunction.
  4. XOR. difference.

Image Averaging

For \(k\) repeated image, \(\bar{g}(x,y)={1\over k}\sum_{i=1}^kg_i(x,y)\)

Filtering

Low-pass spatial filter

Average filter. Blurring, noise reduction. \[1/9*\matrix{1&1&1\\1&1&1\\1&1&1}\]

Median Filter

converge. Choose the median over a spatial filter.

Sharpening

\[\matrix{-1&-1&-1\\-1&8&-1\\-1&-1&-1}\] Result may be negative. Need to scale and/or clip.

Gradient

\(mag(\nabla f)=[({\part f\over \part x})^2 + ({\part f\over \part y})^2]^{1/2}\) can be quickly computed as difference between neighbor pixels,

Prewitt

\[\matrix{-1&0&1\\-1&0&1\\-1&0&1}\] for vertical or its transpose for horizontal.

Sobel

\[\matrix{-1&0&1\\-2&0&2\\-1&0&1}\] for vertical or its transpose for horizontal.

Enhancement in the Frequency Domain

2D DFT and IDFT

\[F(u,v)={1\over MN}\sum_{x=0}^{M-1}\sum_{y=0}^{N-1}f(x,y)e^{-j2\pi(ux/M+vy/N)}\\f(x,y)=\sum_{x=0}^{M-1}\sum_{y=0}^{N-1}F(u,v)e^{j2\pi(ux/M+vy/N)}\]

Filtering

Notch filter

\(H(u,v) = 0\) if \((u,v)=(0,0)\) otherwise \(H(u,v) = 1\) The origin can also locate at (M/2, N/2) for a M*N image matrix

Lowpass filter

mainly background.

  1. Ideal filter
  2. Butterworth Lowpass Filter (BLPF) \[H(u,v) ={1\over1+[D(u,v)/D_0]^{2n}}\]
    • slight variation
    • \[H(u,v) ={1\over1+\alpha[D(u,v)/D_0]^{2n}}\]
  3. Gaussian Lowpass Filter (GLPF) \[H(u,v) = e^{-D^2(u,v)/2D_0^2}\]

Highpass filter

mainly outline.

  1. Ideal filter

  2. Butterworth Highpass Filter (BHPF) \[H(u,v) ={1\over1+[D_0/D(u,v)]^{2n}}\]

    • slight variation

      \[H(u,v) ={1\over1+\alpha[D_0/D(u,v)]^{2n}}\]

  3. Gaussian Highpass Filter (GHPF) \[H(u,v) = 1- e^{-D^2(u,v)/2D_0^2}\]

  4. High Frequency Emphasis for \(0\le a\le1,\ b>a\)\[H(u,v) = a + b{1\over1+[D_0/D(u,v)]^{2n}}\]

Image Power

\[P(u,v)=R(u,v)^2 + I(u,v)^2\\\beta = \sum_u\sum_v{P(u,v)\over P_{total}}\]

Noise

  3. Gamma Random Noise
  4. Exponential Random Noise
  5. Uniform Random Noise
  6. Impulse (salt-and-pepper) Random Noise
  7. Periodic Noise

Spatial Filter

Mean Filters

for a filter in area S and \((s,t) \in S_{xy}\)

  1. Arithmetic mean filter \[f(x,y) = {1\over mn}\sum g(s,t)\]
  2. Geometric mean filter \[f(x,y) = [\Pi g(s,t)]^{1\over mn}\]
  3. Harmonic mean filter \[f(x,y) = {mn\over \sum {1\over g(s,t)}}\]
  4. Contraharmonic mean filter \[f(x,y) = {\sum g(s,t)^{Q+1}\over \sum g(s,t)^Q}\] Q is called the order of the filter
    • \(Q>0\) eliminates pepper noise.
    • \(Q<0\) eliminates salt noise.
    • \(Q=0\) becomes arithmetic mean filter.
    • \(Q=1\) becomes harmonic mean filter.

Order Statistics Filters

  1. Median filter
  2. Max and min filters
  3. Midpoint filter \(0.5*(max+min)\)
  4. Alpha-trimmed mean filter delete \(d/2\) lowest and \(d/2\) highest and get the mean of remaining.

Adaptive Filters

  1. adaptive local noise reduction filter \(\sigma_\eta^2\) is the variance of the noise. \(m_L,\sigma_L^2\) is the mean and variance for the filter. \[f(x,y)=g(x,y) - {\sigma_\eta^2\over\sigma_L^2} [g(x,y)-m_L]\]
  2. adaptive median filter
    1. increase the window size until \(Z_{min} < Z_{med} < Z_{max}\) or size reach the max size.
    2. only set the pixel to median is it is the max or the min.

Frequency Domain Filter

Bandreject and Bandpass Filters

Band filter remove/pass a band of frequencies about the origin of the frequency representation, with width \(W\) and raidal center \(D_0\). Two types of filter have the relation \(H_{bp}(u,v) = 1 - H_{br}(u,v)\).

  1. Ideal band reject.
  2. Butterworth band reject \[H(u,v) ={1\over1+[WD_0/D(u,v)-D_0^2]^{2n}}\]
  3. Gaussian band reject \[H(u,v) = 1- e^{(D_0^2-D^2(u,v))/2WD(u,v)^2}\]

Notch Filters

Notch filter remove/pass frequencies in a predefined neighborhood about a center frequency \((u_0,v_0)\). Two types of filter have the relation \(H_{np}(u,v) = 1 - H_{nr}(u,v)\)

  1. Ideal notch reject \(H_{nr}(u,v)=0\) if \(D_1(u,v)\le D_0\) or \(D_2(u,v)\le D_0\) where \(D_1=\sqrt{(u-u_0)^2+(v-v_0)^2}\) and \(D_2=\sqrt{(u+u_0)^2+(v+v_0)^2}\)
  2. Butterworth notch reject \[H_{nr}(u,v) ={1\over1+[D_0^2/D(u,v)D_0]^{n}}\]
  3. Gaussian notch reject \[H_{nr}(u,v) = 1- e^{-(D_1^2(u,v)D_2^2(u,v))/2D(u,v)^2}\]

Degradation Function

Inverse filtering

given the degradation function H. \(F'(u,v)=F(u,v) + {N(u,v)\over H(u,v)}\)

Minimum mean square error (Wiener) filtering

Assumptions:

  1. noise not related to true image
  2. noise has zero mean
  3. degradation function known as \(H(u,v)\) is linear
  4. power spectrum of the noise \(S_\eta(u,v) =|N(u,v)|^2\) is known
  5. power spectrum of the truth \(S_f(u,v) =|F(u,v)|^2\) is known

\(H*\) is the conjugate transpose of \(H\), meaning conjugate and transpose.

Geometric Transformation

  1. Spatial Transformation \(x' = c_1x+c_2y+c_3xy+c_4\\y' = c_5x+c_6y+c_7xy+c_8\) 4 points to solve
  2. Grey-level Assignments
    • nearest neighbor

Non-linear Filtering

  • Concept of diffusion
    1. mass flux is proportional to the concentration gradient
    2. the rate of flux is is proportional to the concentration gradient
  • Equation
    1. 1D \({\part u\over\part} t = D{\part^2u\over\part x^2}\)
    2. 2D \({\part u\over\part t} = div(D \nabla u)\)

Linear Diffusion Filtering

assume \(D=I\)

\[{\part u\over\part t} = {\part^2 u\over\part x^2}\]

Initial \(u(x,t=0)=I(x)\) If both left and right flow to mid,

\[u(x,t+\Delta t) = u(x,t)+{\Delta t\over(\Delta x )^2}(u(x,t-\Delta t)-2u(x,t)+u(x,t+\Delta t))\]

Morphological Operation

Binary Images

Dilation

extend \(A{\oplus}B\)

Erosion

shrink \(A\ominus B\)

Opening

first erosion then dilation \(A\circ B = (A\ominus B)\oplus B\)

Closing

first dilation then erosion \(A\bullet B = (A\oplus B)\ominus B\)

Boundary Extraction

\(\beta(A) =A-(A\ominus B)\) where B is a small mask.

Region Filling

Iterative process.

\(X_k = (X_{k-1}\oplus B)\and A^C\) where \(B=\matrix {0&1&0\\1&1&1\\0&1&0}\)

Connected Components

Iterative process.

\(X_k = (X_{k-1}\oplus B)\and A\) where \(B=\matrix {1&1&1\\1&1&1\\1&1&1}\)
