Nonhomogeneous Equations

A nonhomogenous equation is

We can solve it by thee steps:

  1. Find the general solution $c_1y_1(t) +c_2y_2(t)$ of the corresponding homogenous equation.
  2. Find some single solution $Y(t)$ of the nonhomogenous equation. Often this solution is referred to as a particular solution.
  3. Form the sum of the functions found in steps 1 and 2.

Method of Undetermined Coefficients

Make an initial assumption about the form of the particular solution $Y(t)$. But with the coefficients left unspecified.
e.g. for $y’’-3y’-4y=3e^{2t}$ we guess that $Y(t)=Ae^{2t}$ and finally obtain $A=-{1\over 2}​$
